# N2O — The Trade

> **This is v1.** It will evolve with your feedback and what we learn from running it. The bar and the structure start now; the details will get sharper over time.

There are roughly 650 CS majors in each UVA class. About 325 clear the GPA bar for Tier-1 internships. Being smart and "UVA CS" is not enough to stand out.

N2O is designed to be an exceptional accelerant for your career. The people who lean in and hold themselves to a high standard get disproportionate results.

---

## What you get

**Our goals for you:**

1. Give you the best possible chance at the best possible job offers.
2. Have you arrive at those jobs more ready and more capable than your peers.

**We help you get there through:**

| What N2O gives you | What that means at graduation |
|---------------------|-------------------------------|
| **Real production work** for serious organizations (CDAO, LMI, DARPA, major hospitals, large enterprises) | You can say "I shipped production code for [org name]" — not "I did a class project" |
| **Ownership and leadership reps** on real teams under real pressure (as you prove yourself and move up) | You can eventually say "I led a pod / owned a subsystem / ran a delivery" — almost nobody your age can |
| **$100K+ in AI tooling and workflows** built for you by end of year | Our tooling makes you more performant than your peers and most existing engineers — and lets you work through 2x+ more client projects while at N2O, which means more experience, more names, and more stories by the time you leave |
| **Mentoring from Matt** (ex-CTO of Risk at Goldman Sachs) and other senior engineers | You've been calibrated by people who already won at the places you want to work |
| **Career packaging**: resume bullets, interview stories, references, and intros — we're building into our client contracts that generals and executives at the companies you work with write recommendations for students on their projects (contingent on performance) | You walk into recruiting with artifacts, advocates, and named references from real generals and executives — not just a GPA |
| **N2O's brand** — which we are investing heavily in building | "Engineer at N2O" and "N2O vouches for me" become real signals in a stack of 650 resumes |

## What you commit to

| Commitment | Why it matters |
|------------|---------------|
| **25+ hours/week** in active terms, consistently | We staff real client work around your availability — inconsistency breaks trust and delivery |
| **Exceptionally hard work** and higher standards than a typical student role | This is how we win projects over firms 10× our size — and how you build a story worth telling |
| **Self-direction**: you bring a plan, not "what should I do?" | The less force we push through you, the more we trust you with |
| **Quality bar**: if you wouldn't show it to a client, don't show it to us | We can't have leadership checking every line — your work needs to be trustworthy |
| **Escalation SOP**: exhaust your options before pulling someone in | Peer time costs 3×. Manager/exec time costs 10×. Protect it. |
| **Show up on time, prepared, with energy** to every meeting | 15 minutes of prep makes the meeting better for everyone else |
| **Up or out**: grow in skill, judgment, and responsibility — or we part ways | This is what makes the N2O credential meaningful |

---

If your main objective is ease and comfort, there are better options. If your main objective is to maximize how strong your career looks at 22 and how fast it moves after that, this is the trade.

## How this works in practice

This doc is the deal. The rest of the system makes it real:

- **Weekly Planning View** — the template you fill out every week: your calendar, goals, and committed/stretch timelines. This is how you commit.
- **How You're Scored** — the rubric, your 1:1 agendas, bands & gates, and what your band unlocks. This is how we measure and how you get feedback.
- **Commitment Agreement** — the specific commitments both sides put their name to. What you commit to us, and what we commit to you.
