## Cannons vs. Cannonballs

Keith Rabois calls the scarcest people in any company **barrels** — someone who can take an idea from conception to shipped without someone else driving. Everyone else is ammunition. You can add all the ammunition you want, but your output is capped by the number of barrels.

At N2O: **Cannons** are barrels. **Cannonballs** are ammunition. A cannonball is smart but needs to be aimed. A cannon is smart and aims itself.

AI makes this distinction matter more, not less. AI tools are force multipliers — but only if the person using them can decide what to point them at. A cannon with great AI tooling is devastating. A cannonball with great AI tooling is still waiting to be told what to do.

Turning you from a cannonball into a cannon is one of the most important things N2O does for you. This rubric is how we both see which one you are right now.

## The Force Equation

```
                Work Shipped  +  Effect on Peers  +  Effect on N2O
  Value  =  ─────────────────────────────────────────────────────────
              Your Time  +  3× Peer Help Time  +  10× Manager/Exec Time
```

**The numerator** is what you produce: code shipped, teammates made faster, N2O's reputation, capacity, and pipeline strengthened.

**The denominator** is what it costs to get that out of you — and notice the multipliers. Every hour a peer spends unblocking you costs **3×**. Every hour a manager or exec spends pushing you costs **10×**. That time is the most expensive thing in the company.

The goal: **maximize the top, minimize the bottom.** The more you ship and the less force we have to push through you to get it — the more we trust you with.

## The Rhythm

| Cadence | What happens | When |
|---------|-------------|------|
| **Daily** | You commit — you're working your stated blocks, hitting your tasks in Sunsama, and making progress against your weekly plan | Every working day |
| **Weekly** | We confront reality — Monday kickoff (what's the plan), Friday check-in (what landed), Friday 1:1 (where do you stand) | Mon 10am, Fri 10am, Fri 1:1 |
| **Biweekly** | We calibrate — you self-score, your lead scores you, you compare in your performance 1:1 | Every 2 weeks |
| **Termly** | We reward or remove — promotions, more scope, stronger advocacy for those hitting the bar; exit for those who aren't | End of term |

## Weekly: 1:1 with Your Lead (15 minutes)

*Fridays, batched around the 10am group check-in. This is where accountability actually happens.*

15 minutes. Same agenda every time. You and your lead look at the numbers, talk about what's working and what isn't, and you leave with one thing to change.

### Agenda (never changes)

**1. Review your week** *(2 min)*

Your lead reviews:

- Hours vs. commitment
- Goal hit rates (committed and stretch timelines)
- Current band (Elite / High / Standard / At Risk)

**2. Three questions** *(10 min)*

| # | Question |
|---|----------|
| 1 | "What went well this week?" |
| 2 | "What sucked / where did you fall short?" |
| 3 | "What's the one behavior you'll change next week?" |

**3. Update your one-line commitment** *(3 min)*

Write it down in your feedback doc before you leave:

> **"This week I will** [specific behavior], **measured by** [simple metric]."

Example: *"This week I will submit all PRs at least 2 hours before my stated committed timeline, measured by PR timestamps vs. my goal sheet."*

That's it. No status theater. Just: here's the score, here's what changes.

## Biweekly: Self-Score Rubric

> ### If the only job offer you ever got was based on how you showed up at N2O these last 2 weeks, would you get hired?

That's the standard. Everything below is evidence for that answer.

**Before scoring, fill in these fields:**

**What did you ship?** What moved because you were here these last 2 weeks? Features, fixes, docs, designs, systems — concrete output, not activity.

____________________

**Goal hit rates** *(pull from your Weekly Planning View)*

- Committed timeline hit rate: _____% *(below 90%? why?)*
- Stretch timeline hit rate: _____% *(50–80% = right zone. 100% every cycle = not aggressive enough.)*

---

For each question, score yourself: **Always (4) / Often (3) / Sometimes (2) / Rarely (1)**

Your lead scores you on the same questions. If your scores are close, it means you see yourself clearly. If they're far apart, that gap is the first thing we talk about.

### CORE DIMENSIONS (1–6)

Core dimensions must be ≥ 3.0 to be considered for Cannon status. A serious weakness in any core dimension blocks you from High or Elite — no matter how good your other scores look.

| # | Dimension | Ask yourself | Score |
|---|-----------|-------------|-------|
| 1 | **Quality & Trust** | Was everything I pushed for review the best I could make it — clean enough that leadership could trust it without redoing it? | ____ |
| 2 | **Hours** | Did I hit 25+ hours each week I committed to? | ____ |
| 3 | **Reliability** | Did I deliver what I said I would, when I said I would? | ____ |
| 4 | **Urgency & Intensity** | Did I pick timelines that made me uncomfortable (not just safe/easy ones)? | ____ |
| 5 | **Agency & Self-Direction** | Did I drive my own work — figuring out what matters, starting it, and keeping leadership informed — without waiting to be tasked? | ____ |
| 6 | **Communication** | Did anyone have to ask me for a status update — or did they already know? | ____ |

> **Note:** We also track components of your code performance and workflow usage directly — things like whether you're using the tools and processes you should be, and to their full capacity. That data informs your scores and our 1:1 conversations, but it's tracked by us, not self-scored.

### SUPPORTING DIMENSIONS (7–8)

Supporting dimensions differentiate Elite from Standard. They won't block your band on their own, but they determine how fast you move up.

| # | Dimension | Ask yourself | Score |
|---|-----------|-------------|-------|
| 7 | **Escalation Hygiene** | Did I fully exhaust the Escalation SOP before pulling someone in? | ____ |
| 8 | **Culture & Ownership** | Did I treat N2O's work and reputation as my own? | ____ |

> **Escalation SOP reminder:** Try it yourself → use an LLM → search docs → try multiple approaches → then escalate with written context, options you've considered, and a clear ask. If you skipped steps, you escalated too early.

## Bands & Gates

Average your scores across all dimensions. Then check the core gates.

| Band | Average | Core Gate (Dimensions 1–6) |
|------|---------|----------------------------|
| **Elite** | ≥ 3.5 | AND all core dimensions ≥ 3.5 |
| **High** | ≥ 3.0 | AND all core dimensions ≥ 3.0 |
| **Standard** | ≥ 2.5 | AND no core dimension ≤ 2.0 |
| **At Risk** | < 2.5 | OR any core dimension ≤ 2.0 |

## What Your Band Unlocks

Your band directly determines your projects, your pay, and how hard we go to bat for you.

| Band | Projects | Pay | Advocacy |
|------|----------|-----|----------|
| **Elite** | Hardest, most important projects; leadership consideration | Most financial upside | Strongest references; most aggressive intros to top firms |
| **High** | More ownership and scope next term | Growing upside | Strong references and intros |
| **Standard** | You keep your seat; coaching continues | — | Standard references |
| **At Risk** | Intensive coaching. Two At Risk terms = exit. | — | — |

*Specific unlocks tied to bands and tenure will be detailed in future documents.*
